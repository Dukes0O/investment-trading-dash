# M3 work order — cycle diagnostics

Issued 2026-07-22 by the architect (Fable 5). Builder: Codex. Prerequisite:
M2-review items 1 and 2 are fixed, re-verified (replay re-run, actions
document regenerated), and committed together with M2.

**Purpose.** The long/flat baseline beat SPY buy-and-hold in only 5 of 24
folds, and the symmetric short mirror was rejected. Before spending any
parameter budget on a "better" strategy (faster re-entry, ratcheting stops,
per-asset shorts), measure exactly where the current baseline loses a full
market cycle: getting out late, getting back in late, or whipsawing in
between. M3 produces measurements only. **No strategy changes, no new
strategy parameters, no holdout access.**

## Primary scope: `trendlab diagnose`

New subcommand. For each configured symbol, over the full pre-holdout
period (not per fold): run the registered long/flat baseline and
buy-and-hold with production costs, then compute the episode and whipsaw
diagnostics below. Register the run in `experiments.jsonl` (kind
`diagnostics`, verdict `recorded`) with a manifest containing every number.
Pure computation lives in `engine/trendlab/backtest/diagnostics.py`.

### Episode detection

Detect major decline episodes on the buy-and-hold equity curve (raw prices
plus distributions, the same accounting as `buy_and_hold`):

- Track the running peak. An episode begins at peak date `P` when equity
  subsequently falls at least `major_drawdown_threshold_pct` (new config in
  `research.yaml`, default 15.0) below the peak.
- Trough date `T`: the minimum equity between `P` and recovery.
- Recovery date `R`: the first date equity closes at or above the `P` peak
  value. If the period ends before recovery, mark the episode
  `incomplete: true` and compute what is computable.
- Episodes are non-overlapping; the next search starts after `R`.

### Per-episode metrics (strategy versus the same dates)

Let `bh(a, b)` and `st(a, b)` be buy-and-hold and strategy total returns
between dates `a` and `b` from their respective equity curves.

- `declineAvoidedPct`: `(bh(P,T) - st(P,T)) / |bh(P,T)| * 100`. 100 means
  the strategy was flat through the whole decline; negative means it lost
  more than buy-and-hold; above 100 is possible only for a short-capable
  strategy and will not occur for the long/flat baseline.
- `recoveryCapturedPct`: `st(T,R) / bh(T,R) * 100`.
- `exitDelaySessions`: trading sessions from `P` until the strategy is
  first flat after `P` (0 if already flat at `P`; null if never flat before
  `T`).
- `reentryDelaySessions`: sessions from `T` until the strategy is first
  long after `T` (0 if long at `T`; null if never long before `R`).
- `cycleCapturePct`: `st(P,R) / bh(T,R) * 100` — realized gain over the
  full cycle relative to the ideal trend-follower that sat out the entire
  decline and caught the entire recovery. Null for incomplete episodes.

### Whipsaw metrics (full period, not per episode)

From the strategy's trade list, for each consecutive exit → re-entry pair
in the same symbol:

- `whipsawCount`: pairs where the re-entry fill price is above the prior
  exit fill price (the system paid to be out).
- `whipsawCostPct`: sum over those pairs of
  `(reentry_fill / exit_fill - 1) * 100`.
- `avgSessionsOut`: mean sessions between exit and re-entry across all
  pairs.

### Summary numbers (the ones Kyle reads)

Per symbol: `wealthRatio` (strategy final wealth / buy-and-hold final
wealth over the full pre-holdout period), episode table, whipsaw totals,
and mean `declineAvoidedPct` / `recoveryCapturedPct` across complete
episodes. The manifest conclusion must name the binding constraint per
symbol: `late-exit`, `late-reentry`, `whipsaw`, or `none`, chosen by the
largest measured cost, with the arithmetic shown.

### Acceptance

1. Synthetic-series tests with hand-computed expected values: one clean
   rise-fall-recover cycle (all five episode metrics exact), one
   double-dip inside a single episode, one unrecovered decline at period
   end, one whipsaw sequence with a known cost. Use zero costs in synthetic
   tests so expectations are exact.
2. On real data: episodes for SPY must include the 2008-2009 and 2020
   declines and the diagnostics must run for all three symbols without
   touching post-cutoff data (`holdoutEvaluated: false` in the manifest).
3. `trendlab status` gains a `lastDiagnostics` pointer (id and date).
4. Every number in the chat-facing output is reproducible from the
   manifest.

## Secondary scope (carried from M2 review, items 3 and 4)

1. Replay holiday coverage: include calendar Fridays whose week has data
   through its last session even when one or more symbols lack a Friday
   bar, so holiday-shortened weeks and the conservative one-week lag are
   exercised and visible in the summary rather than skipped.
2. Heat-capped action label: decide `DEFER` (schema bump to 2, Node
   validator updated, dashboard/LLM consumers keyed) or document that
   uptrend + AVOID + heat rationale is the canonical encoding. Either way,
   record the decision in `docs/DECISIONS.md`.

Out of scope: any change to entry/exit rules, stops, sizing, universe, or
cadence; anything touching the 18-month holdout; M4 candidate design. The
diagnostics decide where the M4 parameter budget goes; they do not spend it.
