# M4 work order — whipsaw reduction candidates

Issued 2026-07-22 by the architect (Fable 5). Builder: Codex. Prerequisite:
M3 diagnostics (`diagnostics-20260722T232833468850Z`) identified whipsaw as
the binding constraint on GLD and SPY (46 of SPY's 52 re-entries above the
prior exit, 177pp cumulative; GLD 27 of 31, 98pp) and late-exit on TLT.
Baseline wealth ratios versus buy-and-hold: GLD 0.535, SPY 0.620, TLT 1.071.

**Objective.** Find a configuration that keeps most of the baseline's
drawdown protection while giving back far less terminal wealth, primarily
by cutting whipsaw. Three candidate families, tested independently against
the unchanged `trend-30w-v1` baseline. The registered baseline is never
modified.

## Pre-registered gates (identical for every family, stated before results)

A candidate passes the pre-holdout screen only if ALL hold:

1. **Wealth**: full-period pre-holdout wealth ratio versus buy-and-hold
   >= 0.85 on at least two of three assets, and above the baseline's wealth
   ratio on all three.
2. **Protection**: full-period maximum drawdown <= 60% of buy-and-hold's
   maximum drawdown on every asset (same equity conventions for both
   curves).
3. **Churn**: cumulative whipsaw cost (M3 definition) <= 50% of the
   baseline's on GLD and SPY.
4. **Folds**: beats the baseline's total return in a majority of
   walk-forward folds aggregated across assets, and per-asset in at least
   half of each asset's folds.
5. **Holdout**: evaluated exactly once, only for a candidate that passed
   1–4, and must not degrade its pre-holdout wealth ratio by more than 15
   percentage points there.

Trial accounting: every configuration lands in `experiments.jsonl`
including failures. Hard cap: **6 configurations per family, 18 total**.
If nothing passes, that is a recorded finding, not a license to widen the
grid. Diagnostics (`cycle_diagnostics`) run for every configuration so the
whipsaw and recovery numbers are always comparable to M3.

## Family E1 — exit hysteresis (parameter budget: 1)

Same strategy as the baseline except the exit requires confirmation:

- E1a (0 parameters): exit only after TWO consecutive completed weekly
  closes below the 30-week SMA.
- E1b (1 parameter, band): exit only when the completed weekly close is
  more than `band_pct` below the 30-week SMA. Grid: 1%, 2%, 3%.

Entry rule unchanged. Register as `trend-30w-hyst-v1`. Economic rationale:
noise around the regime line mean-reverts; demanding displacement or
persistence filters noise trades whose expected cost the M3 whipsaw table
measured directly.

## Family E2 — regime/execution separation with ratcheting stop (budget: 3)

The layered design from the project spec, and Kyle's tighten-not-exit
proposal:

- Regime layer: long-permitted iff completed weekly close > 30-week SMA
  and 10-week SMA > 30-week SMA (the baseline's entry condition, reused,
  zero new parameters).
- Entry: on regime turning long-permitted, at next open (as baseline).
- Exit: NO moving-average exit. A chandelier trailing stop from the
  highest close since entry at `trail_mult` x ATR(14), checked intrabar
  with gap-through fills at the open (mechanics already in the simulator).
  When the weekly close is below the 10-week SMA while the regime is still
  long-permitted (the WEAKENING read), the trail tightens to
  `tight_mult` x ATR.
- Re-entry after a stop-out while the regime remains long-permitted: next
  weekly decision, at next open.

Grid: trail_mult in {4, 5}, tight_mult in {2, 2.5}, capped at 6 configs
total (drop one combination, state which and why in the manifest).
Register as `trend-regime-trail-v1`.

## Family E3 — ride with a catastrophe brake (budget: 1)

The minimal-trading pole of the frontier, testing whether almost never
trading beats trading badly:

- Enter on the baseline entry condition.
- Exit ONLY on a wide trailing stop from the highest close since entry:
  `brake_mult` x ATR(14), grid {6, 8}, or an equivalent fixed-percent
  trail if ATR at these multiples exceeds 25% of price (state the
  conversion in the manifest).
- No moving-average exit, no tightening. Re-entry as E2.

Register as `trend-ride-brake-v1`. Expected prior (state in the manifest):
highest wealth ratio and highest drawdown of the three families; it brackets
the frontier from the low-churn side.

## Reporting

One summary table across all configurations: wealth ratio, max drawdown
versus buy-and-hold, whipsaw cost versus baseline, trades, fold wins,
gates passed. The manifest names the best surviving configuration per
family, or records that none survived. No promotion in M4: promotion
review (including the single holdout evaluation and Kyle's sign-off) is
its own step after Kyle reads the table.

Out of scope: cross-sectional asset selection (M5 candidate), short side,
intraday data, equities cohort, any change to risk limits or the weekly
cadence. Execution-layer entries remain weekly next-open in M4; resting
intra-week entry orders are a separate later experiment so E2's effect is
attributable to the exit change alone.
